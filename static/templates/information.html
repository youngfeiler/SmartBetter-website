Model Information

Historical MLB Dataset

Our model is trained on 3 past seasons of MLB data. The data primarily consists of moneyline, head-to-head pricing in the MLB market. It trains to identify high-quality price discrepancies -- differences between what the market thinks an outcome should be priced and the price at which a sportsbook is offering that outcome.

The dataset is first filtered for +EV bets. These by themselves are profitable in the long run -- it's like getting 3:1 on 'red' at the roulette table when the probability is just around 50%. The difference between SmartBetter and other +EV betting sites is that our model trains to boost hit rate on those +EV bets. Every time you win a +EV bet, you gain a winner and remove a loser, increasing money on money return dramatically.

Example

Lets look at an example: The LA Dodgers are one of the biggest market teams in the MLB. This implies that they have more fans to bet on their games. Therefore, to keep their books balanced, DraftKings might artificially increase the odds for their opponent, attracting customers whose betting inflows can be used to payout Dodgers betters should they win. Our dataset is full of examples like this (100k+). Our model trains to identify patterns within all of these observations and learns to identify which are high quality: it gains a few percentage points on the implied probability of games, increasing the overall +EV of each bet it predicts will win.
